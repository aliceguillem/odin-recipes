Tartiflette

Introduction

Tartiflette is the French culinary bear hug you didn’t know you needed. Imagine potatoes, crispy tofu, and onions, all smothered in a glorious amount of reblochon cheese. It's like the French looked at a potato bake and said, "This needs more cheese and a dash of attitude." Perfect for cheese enthusiasts.

Total cooking preparation:

  • preparation: 20 minutes;
  • total: 45-55 minutes;

Ingredients:

Quantity for 4

  • 500g (about 1.1 lbs) of potatoes
  • 200g (about 7 oz) of firm tofu
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil or another vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 200ml (about 7 fl oz) of crème fraîche
  • 1 reblochon cheese (about 450g or 1 lb)
  • A handful of fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°C (about 400°F).
  2. Boil the sliced potatoes in salted water until just tender, then drain.
  3. While the potatoes are boiling, heat the oil in a pan and sauté the onion, garlic, and tofu cubes. Season with smoked paprika, salt, and pepper, and cook until the tofu is golden and the onions are soft.
  4. In a baking dish, layer half the potatoes, followed by the tofu-onion mixture, and then another layer of potatoes. Spread the crème fraîche (or vegan alternative) evenly over the top.
  5. Arrange slices of reblochon cheese (or vegan cheese) on top, covering the crème fraîche.
  6. Bake for about 20 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving, if desired.
